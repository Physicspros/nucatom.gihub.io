Balanced and Unbalanced Forces

What is force?

Have you ever wondered what causes the stationary truck to move? Why do all the planets revolve around sun? Why does an object start movie? Why does the speed of object change with time? Why do all the planets revolve around the sun? The answer of These question is Force. As you may have already learned force is any push or pull which tries to change the shape, size, state of the object. Force can bring a stationary object at rest and an object at rest in motion. Sometimes forces may be acting a body but it's effect may zero. let us understand the balanced and unbalacned forces Let's first understand about the balanced and unbalanced forces.

What are the balanced forces?

When two equal and opposite forces act on the same object at one point, their net effect is zero this is called as balance force.
For example the forces acting on the car will cancel each other so there effect will be zero.

Fnet = F1 - F2
or
Fnet = F2 - F1

What is unbalanced force?

When the sum of two or more forces acting on the object is not zero, then the forces are called as unbalanced forces.
For example the forces acting on the ball will not cancel each other so the net force acting on the ball will not be zero.

What is net force?

In tug-of-war force acts on the rope at both the ends but the rope does not move in any direction, though same rope can be easily pulled by any of us. So, where is the all force going which is acting on the rope? To answer the question you can see the direction of force at both the ends. The two teas they are applying force opposite to each other. If the forces are equal and opposite they cancel each other. Rope moves in the direction of team which applies more force. The difference of opposite forces is called net force. When forces are in same direction, net force can be found by adding two or more forces acting on the object.

If two forces F1 and F2 act on an object then the net force

Fnet = F1 + F2

In case of opposite forces

Fnet = F1 - F2 or Fnet = F2 - F1

Question1: Two forces of 5 Kg from left and 20 kg from right are acting on an stationary object. Calculte the net force on the object.

Answer:

As given in the question, two forces are oppsite to each other. If 5N is positive the 20N would be negaitve.
Fnet = 5 - 20 = - 15 N.
Here negative sign in the answer shows that 15N force is opposite to 5N force.

Question 2: The engine of car supplies force of 300N in forward direction on the car and friction between the typre and road is 50N in backward direction. Calculate the net force acting on the car.

Answer:

Two forces given in the question, friction and force by engine, are oppsite to each other.

If 300N is positive the 50N would be negaitve.

Fnet = 300 - 50 = 250 N.

In the answer we do not see the negative sign. It means net force is in direction of bigger force which is 300N force.

Question 3: Two forces of 15N and 20N from right are acting on an stationary object. Calculte the net force on the object.

Answer:

As given in the question, two forces are acting in same direction. Both 15N and 20N are positive.

Fnet = 15 + 20 = 35 N.
