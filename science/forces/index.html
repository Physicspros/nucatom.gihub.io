What are the balanced forces?

When two equal and opposite forces act on the same object at one point, their net effect is zero this is called as balance force. For example the forces acting on the car will cancel each other so there effect will be zero. so they are not effective

What is net force?

In tug-of-war force acts on the rope at both the ends but the rope does not move in any direction, though same rope can be easily pulled by any of us. So, where is the all force going which is acting on the rope? To answer the question you can see the direction of force at both the ends. The two teas they are applying force opposite to each other. If the forces are equal and opposite they cancel each other. Rope moves in the direction of team which applies more force. The difference of opposite forces is called net force. When forces are in same direction, net force can be found by adding two or more forces acting on the object. If two forces F1 and F2 act on an object then the net force Fnet = F1+ F2 In case of opposite forces Fnet = F1- F2 or Fnet = F2-F1
